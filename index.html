Australian Mines

There are mines located in all states of Australia with Western Australia, Queensland and New South Wales having the most mines. Both Queensland and New South Wales focus on base metals and also coal. Whereas Western Australia focusses on gold, base metals and iron.

We can also see that most mines in Australia are located near sources of water, including the ocean, rivers and lakes. This is because mines require alot of water during the mining process. They will need water to seperate minerals, cooling machines and controlling dust. Due to the reasons stated above, mines are usually located near water sources.

State Expenditures

Western Australia is the state with most expense, with Queensland and New South Wales alternating between 2 and 3. This is because they have the most mines. Other than that, South Australia, Northern Territory and Victoria alternating between 4-6 place. Lastly, Tasmania rank last for expenses in every year. This is due the small area and small number of mines available in Tasmania. So we can see the higher the number of mines, the higher the expenses.

Each state focuses on different categories of minerals, with most of them having 3-4 different categories. Gold, Base Metals, and Iron are the minerals that have the highest expenditures. This is possibly due to market demand or availability of resources in those regions.

Western Australia, South Australia and Queensland show significantly higher levels of investment compared to states like Tasmania and the Nothern Territory . This trend is aligned with the states' rich resource deposits and mining infrastructure.
